BOOST

Cambridge University Press & Assessment

November 2024

• Built an LLM-powered chatbot that helps English learners write better. It integrates 40 Cambridge Write&Improve tasks and is essentially a very patient, very tireless tutor that never judges your comma splices.

• Powered by TAG, RAG, scaffolded dialogue, multi-stage prompting, and rubric-augmented feedback. That's a lot of acronyms, but each one pulled its weight.

• Pilot deployment saw 22% higher task completion rates. Turns out people write more when something actually helps them instead of just underlining things in red.

Contextual Transformer AES

University of Edinburgh (a.k.a. "the dissertation")

April 2023 - August 2023

• Fed 12,876 essays to RoBERTa, DeBERTaV3, and Longformer to find out if context matters in automated essay scoring. Spoiler: it very much does.

• Achieved 0.823 QWK across all prompts, peaked at 0.8697 in intra-set training. For the uninitiated, that's "the model agrees with human markers more than human markers agree with each other" territory.

• Outperformed state-of-the-art transformers. The models were so good at scoring essays that I briefly considered letting one write my dissertation too.
